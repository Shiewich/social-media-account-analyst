# Platform Fields

Use this reference when mapping exported columns, screenshots, or manually entered metrics.

## Shared Inputs

Ask for these when missing:

- Account: account name, platform, follower count, account positioning, target audience, main monetization path.
- Post-level data: title, publish time, content type, tags/topics, exposure/impressions, reads/views/plays, likes, favorites/saves, comments, shares, follows gained.
- Optional but valuable: cover click rate, average watch duration, completion rate, 2s/3s/5s retention, profile visits, search traffic, recommendation traffic, user demographics.

## Xiaohongshu

Typical column meanings:

- Exposure / impressions: how much the note entered feeds, search, or recommendations.
- Reads / views: actual clicked/opened reading volume.
- CTR: reads divided by exposure. Use it to judge title, cover, and topic packaging.
- Likes: emotional agreement or social approval.
- Favorites / saves: utility value, future-use value, shopping/learning intent.
- Comments: resonance, controversy, question density, or community pull.
- Shares: external usefulness, social currency, or strong identity value.
- Follows gained: account-positioning conversion, not just content popularity.

Important ratios:

- CTR = reads / exposure.
- Interaction rate = (likes + favorites + comments + shares) / reads.
- Utility ratio = favorites / likes. High values usually mean the note solves a practical problem.
- Follower conversion per 1000 reads = follows gained / reads * 1000.
- Search value signal = favorites + comments with question words + evergreen keywords.

Xiaohongshu diagnosis:

- High exposure + high CTR + high saves: scale and serialize.
- High exposure + low CTR: topic is being distributed, packaging is weak.
- High CTR + low exposure: topic/cover works for a small audience; test adjacent keywords.
- High likes + low saves: emotional content, weak utility. Add templates, checklists, steps, or examples.
- High saves + low follows: useful single note but weak account identity. Add stronger creator POV and series framing.

## Douyin

Typical column meanings:

- Plays: distribution volume and content entry into recommendation flow.
- 2s/3s/5s retention: opening hook strength.
- Average watch duration: content-density and pacing quality.
- Completion rate: short-video retention and narrative closure.
- Likes: emotional approval.
- Comments: social trigger, debate, curiosity, or creator-audience relation.
- Shares: social currency and forwarding value.
- Follows gained: account-positioning conversion and creator trust.

Important ratios:

- Interaction rate = (likes + comments + shares + favorites) / plays.
- Follower conversion per 1000 plays = follows gained / plays * 1000.
- Comment rate = comments / plays.
- Share rate = shares / plays.
- Save rate = favorites / plays.

Douyin diagnosis:

- High play + low completion: opening/topic works, content body leaks attention.
- Low play + high completion: content may be good but hook/cover/title or initial distribution is weak.
- High comments + low follows: topic provokes talk but account identity is not clear enough.
- High follows per 1000 plays: use as account-growth seed even if absolute play count is small.
- High share rate: package into broader public conversation or social currency angles.

## Screenshot Inputs

When the user only provides screenshots:

1. Extract visible metrics and labels manually.
2. State which metrics are approximate or unavailable.
3. Use screenshots for qualitative UI/report references, not as complete analytics unless values are readable.
