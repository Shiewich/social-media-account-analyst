# Analysis Framework

Use this reference before writing account conclusions or content strategy.

## Required Analysis Layers

1. Account health: reach, click/open/play, engagement, utility, follower conversion, trend fit.
2. Content-pillar split: infer recurring sections from titles, tags, topics, content format, and user-provided categories.
3. Post archetypes: identify winners, false positives, hidden gems, and stop-loss posts.
4. Platform logic: explain why the same topic should be packaged differently on Xiaohongshu and Douyin.
5. Current trend alignment: connect each pillar to live platform/industry/user-life trends.
6. Next-action plan: recommend what to publish next, what to test, and what to stop.

## Content Pillar Split

If the user has named pillars, preserve them. Otherwise infer pillars from repeated words, title patterns, hashtags, topics, cover style, and audience intent.

Default pillar labels for AI/creative-studio accounts:

- AI tools and efficiency
- Case studies and visual work
- Tutorials and methods
- Industry trends and viewpoints
- Studio behind-the-scenes and growth
- Client/business conversion

For each pillar, decide its role:

- Traffic pillar: brings reach but may not convert.
- Trust pillar: proves expertise and converts followers or clients.
- Utility pillar: earns favorites/search traffic and long-tail discovery.
- Community pillar: generates comments and creator-audience relationship.
- Monetization pillar: leads to inquiries, purchases, or service demand.
- Experiment pillar: not proven yet but matches current trends or strategic direction.

## Post Archetypes

Classify top and bottom posts:

- Perfect match: high reach, strong interaction, and strong follower conversion.
- Viral fluke: high reach but weak conversion or weak account fit.
- Hidden gem: low reach but high conversion, saves, comments, or completion.
- Packaging problem: topic has signal, but CTR/opening/cover/title is weak.
- Body problem: hook works, but retention/completion or interaction is weak.
- Account-fit problem: users like the post but do not remember or follow the creator.

## Extreme Post Investigation

When a post is an outlier, do not explain it only from backend metrics. Investigate why it moved.

Trigger web research when a post has any of these signals:

- Top 10% exposure, views, shares, comments, or follower conversion.
- Bottom 10% CTR, completion, interaction, or follower conversion among posts with meaningful exposure.
- A single post contributes more than 30% of account reach.
- The title contains known IP, celebrities, brands, competitions, games, holidays, public events, school/work nodes, or platform memes.
- The user says a title references another IP, trend, or meme.

Research checklist:

1. Search the exact title, key phrase, brand/IP name, and platform phrase.
2. Check whether public results are reliable. If the exact title is not publicly indexed, say so.
3. Identify whether the signal came from: existing IP fanbase, meme phrase, current event, platform trend, strong visual novelty, emotional timing, brand recognition, or controversy.
4. Separate creator-owned assets from borrowed/licensed/fan IP. Do not call a borrowed IP an original IP.
5. Add commercial-risk notes when content depends on copyrighted, trademarked, or fan/derivative IP. Recommend using it as capability proof or fan-style trend content unless authorization exists.
6. Connect the finding to the next strategy: repeat the mechanism, not the exact copyrighted element.

When a post borrows a mature IP such as Pokémon, treat high reach as "IP leverage + visual execution" rather than pure account-product-market fit. Recommend converting the attention into creator identity, behind-the-scenes proof, or authorized/original IP development.

## Trend Scan Protocol

Do a fresh scan when the user asks for current strategy, next topics, or "当下热点".

Collect 7-30 day signals from:

- Platform hot lists, search suggestions, trending hashtags, creator-center insights, and ad/brand trend pages.
- Competitor posts in the same niche, especially recent high-engagement posts.
- Macro context: AI adoption, creator economy, local lifestyle, consumption confidence, work/career pressure, new tools, policy/platform shifts, seasonal events, festivals, school/work cycles.
- Comment sections: repeated questions, objections, requests for templates, and emotional vocabulary.

Score each trend for each pillar:

- Relevance: does it match the account's audience and positioning?
- Evidence: is there real platform/user signal, not just a vague buzzword?
- Differentiation: can the account say something non-generic?
- Feasibility: can the team produce it quickly and consistently?
- Conversion: will it attract the right followers, clients, or community?

Use trend fit to shape strategy, not to chase every hot word. Avoid recommending trends that damage account positioning.

## Recommendation Format

For each recommended next post, include:

- Pillar and strategic reason.
- Topic and one-sentence promise.
- Xiaohongshu packaging: title, cover text, opening paragraph, note structure, search keywords.
- Douyin packaging: first 3 seconds, shot rhythm, conflict/question, middle proof, ending CTA.
- Expected metric to watch: CTR, completion, saves, comments, follows per 1000, or inquiry leads.
- Why now: current trend or audience-timing reason.

## Final Report Structure

Use this order:

1. Executive conclusion.
2. Account health dashboard.
3. Content-pillar map.
4. Top wins and hidden gems.
5. Problems and causes.
6. Current trend opportunities.
7. Next 3-5 content actions.
8. 2-week testing plan.
9. Data gaps and next export checklist.
